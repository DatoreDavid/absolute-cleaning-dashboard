Understanding Data & Analytics

What is Data in a Business Context?

Data is any information that can be collected, stored, and analysed to help make business decisions. In facilities management, data comes from everywhere.

Types of Business Data:

‚ö° Operational Data
Service delivery times, completion rates, resource usage
üí∞ Financial Data
Costs, budgets, invoicing, contract values
üìà Performance Data
KPIs, SLAs, quality scores, customer satisfaction
üèóÔ∏è Asset Data
Equipment status, maintenance history, energy consumption
üå°Ô∏è Environmental Data
Temperature, occupancy, air quality, energy usage
üìã Compliance Data
Safety incidents, audit results, regulatory reporting
The Problem with Data Today:

Most organisations have data everywhere but insights nowhere: Excel spreadsheets scattered across teams ‚Ä¢ Different systems that don't talk to each other ‚Ä¢ Manual reporting that's always out of date ‚Ä¢ No single source of truth ‚Ä¢ Reactive decision-making based on gut feeling

What is Analytics?

Analytics is the process of bringing data together to examine and discover patterns, draw conclusions, and make informed decisions. It's turning raw data (from all those places across the business) into actionable insights (insights that lead to decisions being made).

Four Levels of Analytics:

1. Descriptive Analytics - "What happened?"

‚Ä¢ Basic reporting and dashboards

‚Ä¢ Historical performance summaries

Example: "We completed 95% of cleaning tasks last month"

2. Diagnostic Analytics - "Why did it happen?"

‚Ä¢ Root cause analysis

‚Ä¢ Correlation identification

Example: "Cleaning delays were highest on Mondays due to weekend damage"

3. Predictive Analytics - "What will happen?"

‚Ä¢ Forecasting and trend analysis

‚Ä¢ Risk assessment

Example: "HVAC system likely to fail in next 3 weeks based on vibration patterns"

4. Prescriptive Analytics - "What should we do?"

‚Ä¢ Optimisation recommendations

‚Ä¢ Automated decision-making

Example: "Reschedule cleaning team to optimise routes and reduce travel time by 20%"

  • "Are you competing for contracts against the big players like Mitie, Sodexo, or CBRE?"
Current Data Situation
  • "Where do you currently store your operational data - schedules, timesheets, quality checks?"
  • "How many different systems do your teams use day-to-day?" (Looking for 3+ systems)
